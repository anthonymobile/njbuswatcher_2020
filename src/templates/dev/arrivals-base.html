About This Site

Jersey City Heights is the northernmost neighborhood in New Jersey's largest municipality and since the demolition of an extensive network of streetcars in the 1950s has relied extensively on buses for transit connections to surrounding areas and New York City.

Unfortunately, despite service improvements by NJ Transit and the growth of private jitney lines, the area still suffers from transit backups and irregularities. This website draws on real-time data collected from NJ Transit's transit API to track service quality and inform riders on overall trends in performance over time to improve dialogue between the public, community and city leaders, and transit officials.
